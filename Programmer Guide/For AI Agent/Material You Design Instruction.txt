====================================================================
    MATERIAL YOU (M3) DESIGN GUIDE FOR AI AGENTS
====================================================================

PURPOSE OF THIS GUIDE:
--------------------------------------------------------------------
This document is a set of "Rules of Engagement" for any AI Assistant
working on the HOA System's Frontend. 

To call a design "Material You," the AI must follow the logic of
Material Design 3 (M3). This guide explains how to do that in 
simple terms.


====================================================================
    THE CORE ANALOGY: The Chameleon UI 🦎
====================================================================

Think of Material You like a Chameleon. In the past, apps had 
"Hard Colors" (like "This button is always Red #FF0000"). 

In Material You, the UI "samples" its environment. If you pick a
single Seed Color, the UI creates a whole family of related 
shades (Tonal Palettes) that always match.


====================================================================
    THE 4 PILLARS OF M3 (FOR AI AGENTS)
====================================================================

If you are an AI designing a component, you MUST check these 4:

1.  COLOR (Tonal, not Flat)
    - Don't use random hex codes. Use Tonal Relationships.
    - Primary: The main brand color.
    - Secondary: Subtle accents.
    - Tertiary: Contrasting accents for highlights.
    - Neutral: For backgrounds and surfaces.
    - Rule: Text on a "Primary" surface must be "On-Primary."

2.  SHAPE (Pills & Rounded Squares)
    - M3 loves curves. 
    - Buttons & Search Bars: Use "Full Rounding" (28px - 999px / Pill shape).
    - Cards & Dialogs: Use "High Rounding" (12px to 24px).
    - NEVER use 0px (sharp) corners unless explicitly asked.

3.  TYPOGRAPHY (The Hierarchy)
    - Use 'Poppins' or 'Inter'.
    - Large Headers: Use "Display" or "Headline" styles.
    - Component Labels: Use "Label Large" (Bold, clean).
    - Long Text: Use "Body Medium."

4.  ELEVATION (Shadows & Surfaces)
    - M3 uses "Tonal Elevation." 
    - Instead of heavy black shadows, use slight color tints (Surfaces)
      to show that something is "floating" or "on top."


====================================================================
    VISUALIZATION: The Restaurant Analogy 🍽️
====================================================================

Imagine a fancy restaurant:
- The "Seed Color" is the mood lighting of the room.
- If the light is Blue, the menus, napkins, and plates all have a 
  slight blue tint so they feel like they belong together.
- MATERIAL YOU is the same: The background isn't just "Grey," it's 
  a very faint version of the Seed Color.


====================================================================
    AI COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST
====================================================================

Before you submit a JSX/CSS change, ask yourself:
[ ] Does this use a "Seed Color" to derive its palette?
[ ] Are my buttons pill-shaped or highly rounded (16px+)?
[ ] Did I use "Tonal Buttons" (tinted background) instead of flat?
[ ] Is the typography using the M3 scale (Display -> Label)?
[ ] Are there "State Layers" (slight color change on hover/focus)?

====================================================================
    FINAL INSTRUCTION
====================================================================
If the design looks "boxed," "sharp," or uses "pure unrelated colors,"
it is NOT Material You. Keep it soft, rounded, and tonally consistent.
